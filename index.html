About Us

JEM's Speakers Club is a non-governmental organization dedicated to promoting **open speaking** and building a supportive environment for people to find and implement **sustainable solutions** to vital issues in society.

Our Vision

"To build a community that promotes the unfolding of secrets, problem-solving, and implementation of sustainable solutions to neglected, sensitive, and vital issues in society."

Our Mission

"To use various creative and developmental activities and programs to motivate and educate people as well as build a supportive environment for them to feel free and/or be close enough to speak openly, be intrinsically motivated, and uphold patriotism, whilst supporting the underprivileged in society."

Our Activities

We engage in a variety of activities to achieve our mission:

Writings

  • Shairi Fikiri: Thought-provoking poems on contemporary issues.
  • Projection Poems: Poems promoting essential social, cultural, and political issues.
  • Inking Heart: Deep reflection poems that reveal the inside, unknown side of individuals or situations.

Shows

  • TOBox: Broadcasts featuring honest youth opinions on education, health, arts, culture, and politics.
  • Brief Talks: A show that inspires and enlightens society on creativity and innovation.
  • Set Talks: A motivational and inspirational program that stirs contemplation and grit.

Programs

  • Youth-Cry: A poetic show discussing the mental, psychological, and spiritual traumas within youth.
  • Vijana-Concerns: A youth-focused forum on SWOT analysis for young people.
  • Speakers Nobel Prize: Awards for individuals with exceptional qualities and exemplary behaviors.

Key Policies

The following are some of the fundamental policies of JEM's Speakers Club, derived from its constitution:

Membership and Conduct

Membership is open to all who adhere to the constitution's principles and are keen on becoming solution-providers for societal issues. All members are expected to uphold the club's code of conduct.

Financial Management

The Club ensures financial accountability through its Treasury. Funds are managed by the Treasurer, overseen by the General Secretary and Executive Director. Sources of funds include donations, sales, and revenue from club activities. The financial year runs from October 1st to September 30th.

Meetings and Amendments

Weekly meetings are held on Wednesdays from 20:00 to 22:00, with schedules communicated by the Executive Director. The constitution can be amended with the approval of at least 70% of the members.
